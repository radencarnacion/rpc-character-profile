Impressed by his dedication to the otherwise mundane investigative craft - and the fact that Janus had actually questioned the god's existence when the deity first appeared before him - Savras blessed the ever-skeptical mage with his favor, granting the man the slivers of divine knowledge that would assist him further in his unyielding search for truth.

Spellbook

With pages meticulously margined and sized, Janus' hardbound, navy blue spellbook is filled with diagrams, charts and case studies, making it read more like an scholar's dissertation as opposed to a personal collection of writings. The one quirk of this book is that all of the text is written in Celestial, despite the arcane formulae adhering to the standing academic theory. Quizzically, the symbol of Savras, the God of Divination and Fate, is engraved on the bottom-left corner of the back cover like a publisher's mark.

Arcane Focus: Looking-Orb Wand

A modern-looking wand, the focus' grip is made of smoothly machined and polished brass. Grooves line the bottom half, aligning with Janus' prefferred grip style of holding it more like a baton rather than a pointing stick. The wand is topped with a small crystal orb, with the orb milled and polished to an extent that anything one sees through it at close proximity is clearly enlarged for ease of inspection.

Coming from a historied lineage of mages, Janus Typhon was immediately thrust into the world of nobility, politics and high society in his youth. Alas, gifted with keen insight, all the pomp and glamor of the wealthy did nothing more than to make him disdain the seemingly natural ability of people to lie, cheat and stab even while bearing the most sincere-looking smile and the sweetest tone of voice one could possibly muster. Rejecting the unspoken rules of nobility, Janus decided to focus his scholarship towards the pursuit of absolute truth: that nothing was to be taken at face value unless it could be proven to be absolutely true. Feigning a gluttonous appetite for arcane knowledge like mages that came before him, Janus convinced his family to let him embark on a venture of discovery, allowing him to meet like-minded folks who could at least sympathize, if not understand, his unending search for truth. Spending years under the tutelage of many sages of their craft, whether magic or mundane, the man began to understand the underpinnings of both the natural and the arcane world. Collecting aged wisdom, Janus eventually found patterns were none sought to look. Old wives' tales became common sense. Common sense became theories. Theories became axioms. Eventually, the man had unlocked foresight beyond the celestial: the ability to predict the immediate future not because of divine intervention, but because of the cycles of mortal history. Once he deemed himself well-prepared, he brought his theses to the attention of the same scholars who had lectured him in his adulthood. While many were skeptical, none were outright dismissive because of their personal experiences with the Janus' years-long quest for truth. Granted a chance to present his findings to the Guild of Mages as a condition of his acceptance, he nonetheless proved his sagacity when he outright solved the murder of one of his would-be judges, proving with logic, investigative tools, modern interrogation methods, and arcane theory that it was one of the other judges listening to his speil at that very moment that was the mastermind behind the killing. Impressed with his fortright use of his knowledge, he was one day visited by none other than an apparition of Savras, the patron god of investigators. In true Janus fashion, the mage questioned the god's very existence, dragging the deity into an hours-long scholarly debate that ultimately resulted in Janus accepting that Savras was a real divine being. Amused, the god blessed the mage and marked the man as his own, coverting the wizard's dissertation into what was essentially a holy scripture of facts, figures and formulae. Nowadays, the man still roams the continents seeking wisdom and truth, though he moves around a lot less frequently due to his duties and responsibilities as a noble member of magecraft. But in whichever city or town he resides, the locals know that if they seek information, or wish a baffling mystery be solved to satisfaction, they simply need to seek out Janus Typhon, the Arcane Investigator.
