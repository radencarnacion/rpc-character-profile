Impressed by his dedication to the otherwise mundane investigative craft - and the fact that Janus had actually questioned the god's existence when the deity first appeared before him - Savras blessed the ever-skeptical mage with his favor, granting the man the slivers of divine knowledge that would assist him further in his unyielding search for truth.

Spellbook

With pages meticulously margined and sized, Janus' hardbound, navy blue spellbook is filled with diagrams, charts and case studies, making it read more like an scholar's dissertation as opposed to a personal collection of writings. The one quirk of this book is that all of the text is written in Celestial, despite the arcane formulae adhering to the standing academic theory. Quizzically, the symbol of Savras, the God of Divination and Fate, is engraved on the bottom-left corner of the back cover like a publisher's mark.

Arcane Focus: Looking-Orb Wand

A modern-looking wand, the focus' grip is made of smoothly machined and polished brass. Grooves line the bottom half, aligning with Janus' prefferred grip style of holding it more like a baton rather than a pointing stick. The wand is topped with a small crystal orb, with the orb milled and polished to an extent that anything one sees through it at close proximity is clearly enlarged for ease of inspection.
